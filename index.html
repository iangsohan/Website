ABOUT ME

My name is Ian Gonzales Sohan. I am a Computer Science major at the University of Virginia. My goal in life is to travel the world and have a family. I plan to accomplish my goals by having an enjoyable career that makes a positive impact.

Education

I am currently a rising third-year student at the University of Virginia. I plan to graduate with a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Sciene along with a minor in Engineering Business. My current career and education goals include remaining a member of the Dean's List for the engineering school, continue to improve my programming skills and discover what fields I find interesting, and be accepted to an internship position as a software engineer.

I went to the Woodstock Academy in my hometown of Woodstock, CT. While attending, I played on the school's soccer team and found a love in wood-working. My most notable accomplishments include being president of the Math Honor Society, a member in the National Honor Society, and a captain for the school's Relay for Life team. Leaving high school, I had no idea what I wanted to do. I thought I would enjoy economics because that's what my dad studied and I enjoyed talking to him about it, but by chance, my first college course was intro to programming and I never looked back.
